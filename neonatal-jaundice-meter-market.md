<p><p>A neonatal jaundice meter is a medical device used to measure the levels of bilirubin in a newborn's blood to diagnose neonatal jaundice. The market is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of neonatal jaundice, advancements in technology, and growing awareness about the importance of early detection and treatment of the condition. Additionally, the rise in birth rates and improvements in healthcare infrastructure in developing countries are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the neonatal jaundice meter market is the development of non-invasive devices that use transcutaneous bilirubinometry to measure bilirubin levels without the need for a blood sample. These devices are convenient for use in neonatal care units and homecare settings, making it easier to monitor and manage neonatal jaundice. Other trends include the integration of digital technologies for data management and analysis, as well as the introduction of portable and handheld devices for point-of-care testing.</p></p>
