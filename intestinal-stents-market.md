<p><p>Intestinal stents are categorized into four main types based on their location in the digestive system: duodenum stents, small intestine stents, colon stents, and rectum stents. Duodenum stents are used to relieve blockages in the first part of the small intestine, small intestine stents are placed in the middle section, colon stents are utilized in the large intestine, and rectum stents are inserted in the final part of the gastrointestinal tract. Each type of stent serves the purpose of maintaining the patency of the respective section of the intestine.</p></p>
