<p><p>Magnetic rotary encoders are sensors that detect and measure the rotational movement of an object. There are two main types of magnetic rotary encoders: Incremental and Absolute. Incremental magnetic rotary encoders provide relative position information by counting pulses as the encoder shaft rotates. Absolute magnetic rotary encoders, on the other hand, provide the exact position of the shaft at any given time, eliminating the need to reset the position upon power-up. Both types have different applications and are used in various industries for accurate position sensing.</p></p>
