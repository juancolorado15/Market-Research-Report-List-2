<p><p>Vascular plugs are medical devices used in interventional radiology to embolize blood vessels. Two common types of vascular plugs include Titanium-Nickel (Nitinol) and Titanium-Nitride (Tin) plugs. Nitinol plugs are made from a shape memory alloy that allows for easy deployment and precise positioning within the blood vessel. Tin plugs provide good visibility under fluoroscopy due to their radiopacity. Both types of vascular plugs are effective in occluding blood vessels and are widely used in various medical procedures to treat conditions such as aneurysms and bleeding.</p></p>
